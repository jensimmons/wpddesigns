WebPlatform.org is a work in progress. We could use your help in making it better. Join us.

Created by a powerhouse coalition. And you.

Web Platform is a project of the W3C and the Web Platform stewards — Adobe, Apple, Facebook, Google, HP, Intel, Microsoft, Mozilla, Nokia, and Opera. It’s a collaboration between those who make the specs, those who build the browsers, and developers from all over the world. Wanna join us?

Open. Free. Crowdsourced.

The web exists because of the open generosity of those who invented it. We share those principles: that by coming together and collaborating, we can make something great for the world to use for free. Web Platform is not run by a single corporation, or a single person. It’s by and for us all. Anyone is welcome to help write and edit the documents. Join the effort.

How to contribute to Web Platform Docs

Step 1: Join the Group Email List and Introduce Yourself.

The first step is to sign up for our email group. Join the list by emailing an empty message with the subject “subscribe” to public-webplatform-request@w3.org from the email address you'd like to use. You can find more, and read the archives, at lists.w3.org/Archives/Public/public-webplatform.

Once you are on the list, please email the group and introduce yourself. Who are you? What would you like to contribute?

Step 2: Jump into chat.

To talk to folks in real time, and get a quick answer to a question, jump into our public chat room. We are using IRC — a type of chatroom that's older than the web. We have a dedicated chatroom (called a “channel”) on the IRC network Freenode.

Server: Freenode (irc.freenode.net)
Channel: #webplatform

Please feel free to enter the IRC channel anytime, and just start talking to whoever’s there. You can connect through an IRC client (standalone software) or this web interface. Here’s more on how to get started.

Step 3: Get a Media Wiki Account and Check Out The Editor’s Guide

Much of the work that’s needed revolves around editing the docs themselves. Currently, all the content about HTML, CSS, Javascript and more is on a Media Wiki website. Anyone can sign-up for an account, which allows you to log in and edit pages. Find information about how to get an account and what work is needed to be done at docs.webplatform.org/wiki/WPD:Editors_Guide.

Write Documentation with Web Platform Wednesdays

“Web Platform Wednesdays” is an effort to organize who’s doing what in the next week. This small group focuses together on editing one particular section of the site, to gain momentum, and get that section done. If you'd like to be involved, email the list and volunteer. You can see the current list of who is editing which page at: docs.webplatform.org/wiki/Meta:web_platform_wednesday.

Attend (or Host) a Doc Sprint.

A great way to join the effort is to attend a doc sprint. Organized by different people around the world, WebPlatform.org doc sprints gather a group of people, in person, to spend the day writing documentation. We’ll help you get setup, walk you through the process, and show you where to start.

To find out if there’s an upcoming doc sprint near you, check the list at docs.webplatform.org/wiki/WPD:Doc_Sprint. If you are interested in organizing a doc sprint in your area, email the list and let us know.
